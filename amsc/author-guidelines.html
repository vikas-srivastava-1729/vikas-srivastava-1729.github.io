Instructions for Authors

Editorial Procedure

Single-Blind Peer Review

The journal follows a single-blind reviewing procedure and publishes special/guest-edited issues. The peer review process for these articles is the same as the journal’s general peer review process.

If a guest editor authors an article in their issue/collection, they will not handle the peer review process.

Editorial Procedure - Additional Information

All submissions undergo an initial completeness check before being assigned to an Editor for suitability assessment. If an Editor is an author or has a competing interest, another Editorial Board member will oversee the review. Editors consider peer-reviewed reports but are not bound by them. A single concern from a reviewer or the Editor may lead to rejection.

Time to First Decision

The journal aims for a quick initial decision. Factors affecting timing include article length, reviewer availability, and complexity of review. Clear language and well-structured content help expedite the process.

Special issues may have different schedules—please check the Call for Papers.

Time to Publication

Accepted articles proceed immediately to production. If proofs are returned promptly, the article should be published online within 3-5 weeks.

Article DOI

Once proofed, articles are published online and assigned a DOI for citation. No further changes can be made after online publication.

Article Charges

TThe journal requires a publication fee for accepted manuscripts. The journal will publish the research works as Open Access, which involves an Article Processing Fee.

Benefits of Open Access

  • Wider Reach: Immediate global access enhances researcher engagement.
  • Greater Visibility: Open Access articles receive four times more views and 1.7 times more citations.

Manuscript Submission

By submitting a manuscript, authors confirm that the work has not been previously published, is not under review elsewhere, and has received approval from all co-authors (if applicable) and the relevant authorities at the institution where the research was conducted. The publisher bears no legal responsibility for any compensation claims that may arise.

Permissions

Authors must secure permission for any figures, tables, or text that have been previously published. If they intend to use such content in their manuscript, they must obtain authorization from the copyright holder(s) for both print and online formats and provide proof of permission upon submission. Any material submitted without proper documentation will be considered the original work of the authors.

Online Submission

Submit manuscripts via the “Submit manuscript” link, following on-screen instructions.

Source Files

All relevant editable source files (e.g., .docx, LaTeX) must be provided at submission and revision.

Title Page

Ensure the title page includes:

  • Concise, informative title
  • Author name(s) and affiliation(s)
  • Corresponding author’s active email
  • 16-digit ORCID (if available)

Statements and Declarations

Competing Interests

Authors must disclose financial or non-financial interests related to the work.

Research Data Policy

The journal follows standard research data policy, encouraging data sharing. Sharing of all relevant research data is strongly encouraged and authors must add a Data Availability Statement to original research articles.

Disclosures and Declarations

Authors are required to provide details on funding sources, any financial or non-financial interests, ethical approval from the relevant committee for studies involving humans and/or animals, informed consent for research involving human participants, and a statement on animal welfare when applicable.
